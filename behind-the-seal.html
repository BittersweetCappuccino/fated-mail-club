From the Studio

Behind the Seal

Every envelope begins in a small, lamplit room in Portland — and ends with a drop of molten wax. This is how a story gets made by hand, and why we still refuse to do it any other way.

It started with a single letter. In the winter of 2022, our founder mailed a hand-written romantasy story to a friend who was going through a hard season — folded into an envelope, closed with a thumbprint of red wax, sent the slow way. Her friend called in tears. No one makes things like this anymore, she said. Make more.

So we did. We rented a studio the size of a generous closet, found a roster of authors who wrote love and magic in equal measure, and started sealing stories one at a time. We never built an app. We never wrote an algorithm. We decided, stubbornly, that the most modern thing we could offer was something entirely analog — a story you could hold, that no screen could interrupt.

Four years later, the studio is a little bigger and the wax stains are a little deeper. But the ritual hasn't changed. Someone still folds every letter. Someone still presses every seal. Your name is still inked by a human hand before your story leaves to find you.

The Craft
Every Seal, by Hand

The wax is real shellac, melted over a small flame and poured warm. We press our brass die — cut from the original 2022 drawing — into each pool by hand, which is why no two seals are ever quite identical. One sits a hair off-center; another catches the light differently. We could machine them perfectly. We never will. The small imperfections are the proof that a person made this, for a person, on purpose.
